## DealerScope Master Audit

### 1. Executive Summary

The DealerScope codebase, while demonstrating strengths in modular design, observability, and robust error handling in certain areas, contains **critical security vulnerabilities and widespread failures in enforcing core business rules**. The most severe issues include hardcoded API keys and webhook secrets in public repositories, unauthenticated access to sensitive financial data, and broken access control that allows unauthorized modification of user data. Furthermore, crucial business logic governing vehicle age, mileage, and rust-state filtering is inconsistently or incorrectly applied across the ingest pipeline, particularly within the Apify scrapers, leading to the processing of non-compliant vehicles. This systemic failure poses direct risks to data security, financial integrity, and business operations, necessitating an immediate halt to deployment until these critical findings are remediated.

| Min gross margin: $1,500 | **PARTIAL** | Checked in `score_deal()` and in the ingest loop, but not consistently on every write path. The calculation of gross margin in `score_deal` is incorrect as it does not include buyer premiums and auction fees, leading to inflated numbers. |

| Max mileage: 50,000 | **PARTIAL** | **Critical bypass.** Enforced only when mileage is present; missing mileage data passes. Some source paths (e.g., government sources) allow much higher caps (up to 200,000). |
| High-rust states rejected | **PARTIAL** | **Critical bypass.** While rust-belt filters exist, several paths widen the bypass to 8, 18, 20, or even 25 years (e.g., for government sources) which is outside the stated rule. Inconsistent implementation across scrapers. |

### 6. Final Verdict

**HOLD**

The DealerScope codebase, in its current state, contains too many **critical security vulnerabilities and fundamental business rule bypasses** to proceed with deployment or dealer onboarding. The exposure of production API keys and webhook secrets, the widespread broken access control, and the systemic failure to enforce core business logic for vehicle eligibility directly threaten the integrity, security, and financial viability of the platform. Immediate and comprehensive remediation of all critical findings is essential before any further development or deployment considerations.
